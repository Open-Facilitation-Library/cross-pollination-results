P2 Donation Collective Choice

1 participants 2026-03-16
The Question
In this study, you will take part in a collective decision about how to allocate a charitable donation. You will be asked to decide which charity should receive a $10 donation. Several charities are working on different approaches to addressing social problems. Each of them uses donations in different ways and focuses on different types of impact. In an anonymous conversation with a neutral AI assistant, you will be asked to rank the options and briefly explain why exactly you prefer your top choice. After everyone in the group makes their choice, the charity that receives the most support will be selected as the winner.
Key Results

This session focused on a collective decision-making exercise where participants ranked charitable organizations to determine which should receive a $10 donation. The conversation centered on one participant's preference and reasoning.

💡 Key Reasoning & Insights

Primary Motivation

  • Personal Connection: Participant1 has direct experience working with street animals, creating a strong emotional and experiential connection to this cause
  • Relatable Impact: The participant finds animal welfare struggles more relatable than other social issues presented

Notable Perspective

  • Skepticism about AI Prioritization: Participant1 expressed concern that the AI assistant might prioritize environmental causes (trees) over both animals and people, finding this "weird"
  • The participant appreciated when this concern was acknowledged as valid
🎯 Session Goal Achievement

🔍 Insights for Collective Decision

Factors Influencing This Vote:

  • Personal experience and direct exposure to the cause
  • Emotional relatability to beneficiaries
  • Preference for tangible, direct impact on living beings
  • Trust concerns about AI neutrality in the process

Decision-Making Style: Experience-based and emotionally-driven rather than analytical or impact-metrics focused

How Cross-Pollination Works
Phase 1

Share your view

Participants answer the question independently, without seeing others' responses.

Between

Ideas collected

An AI facilitator synthesizes all responses into key themes and perspectives.

Phase 2

Hear others

Participants see the synthesis and respond — updating, refining, or reaffirming their views.

Outcome

Collective picture

The final synthesis captures where the group aligns, disagrees, and what shifted.
