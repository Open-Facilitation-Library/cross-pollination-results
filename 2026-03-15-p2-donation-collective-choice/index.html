The Question
In this study, you will take part in a collective decision about how to allocate a charitable donation. You will be asked to decide which charity should receive a $10 donation. Several charities are working on different approaches to addressing social problems. Each of them uses donations in different ways and focuses on different types of impact. In an anonymous conversation with a neutral AI assistant, you will be asked to rank the options and briefly explain why exactly you prefer your top choice. After everyone in the group makes their choice, the charity that receives the most support will be selected as the winner.
Key Results

The group deliberated on allocating a $10 charitable donation among four options: Community Health Clinic, urban tree planting, animal rescue, and food pantry. The discussion centered on identifying the most impactful use of funds in addressing social problems.

Detailed Findings
Overview

The group deliberated on allocating a $10 charitable donation among four options: Community Health Clinic, urban tree planting, animal rescue, and food pantry. The discussion centered on identifying the most impactful use of funds in addressing social problems.

🎯 Final Decision

Participant1's Choice: Community Health Clinic

Key Reasoning:

  1. Healthcare Access Crisis - Identified as a critical, systemic issue in the US with limited alternative solutions
  2. Dual Impact - Serves both immediate patient needs AND provides supervised learning opportunities for medical students
  3. Comprehensive Care - Addresses both physical and mental healthcare needs (mental health aspect added during discussion)
  4. Irreplaceability - "There's only one way to get to go to a doctor" - healthcare access has fewer alternative pathways than other social issues
💡 Key Insights from Discussion

Healthcare Prioritization

  • Healthcare was viewed as fundamentally different from other charitable causes due to its singular access pathway
  • Mental healthcare emerged as an important dimension that strengthened the clinic's case
  • The educational component (training future doctors) was seen as creating long-term systemic value

Comparative Analysis

  • Food Pantry - Deemed less critical in "a large western city with very high salaries" where hunger is "not that big of an issue"
  • Other Options (urban trees, animal rescue) - Acknowledged as useful but not discussed in detail; implicitly ranked lower than healthcare
🔄 Evolution of Thinking

Initial Position: Healthcare access is the primary concern ↓ Mid-Discussion: Mental healthcare dimension added to strengthen the argument ↓ Final Position: Ranking unchanged; comprehensive healthcare (physical + mental) + educational value solidified the choice

📊 Decision Framework Applied

Participant1 evaluated options based on:

  1. Severity of need - How critical is the problem?
  2. Alternative solutions - Can the issue be addressed through other means?
  3. Scope of impact - Who benefits and how?
  4. Systemic value - Does it create lasting change?

The Community Health Clinic scored highest across all dimensions, particularly on irreplaceability and scope of impact.
